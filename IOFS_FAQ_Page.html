Membership — What's Included

What does membership include?

+

Membership includes monthly one-hour educational sessions with Dr. Bandy Lee, M.D., advisory newsletters, forensic analysis, national data insights, and the ability to submit member questions across 15 categories of forensic expertise. All members also receive recording downloads of every session and full access to all past content — regardless of their ability to attend live. All materials are educational and policy-informed only.

Members also have access to our growing content library — including past sessions, newsletters, and educational briefings available for individual purchase à la carte.

If I miss the live monthly meeting, how will I receive access?

+

All members have full access to all content and meetings regardless of their ability to attend live. Every session is recorded and made available as a downloadable file — so no meeting is ever missed.

Will members have full access to previous content including educational meetings, newsletters, and educational content?

+

Yes. All members can purchase content a la carte as part of our library. Every educational session, newsletter, and piece of content is available to members as part of the IOFS content library.

What happens when I submit a question?

+

Questions are reviewed for educational relevance and professional scope. Themes may inform future briefings or reports. Individual responses are not guaranteed, but all submissions are carefully reviewed and considered for inclusion in upcoming sessions and educational content.

Who develops the educational content?

+

Content is informed by forensic psychiatric research, national data, interdisciplinary analysis, and the leadership of Dr. Bandy X. Lee, M.D. — one of the most internationally recognized forensic psychiatrists and public health advocates in the United States.

Who is membership designed for?

+

The Just Science Community is designed for families, pro se litigants, legal professionals, policymakers, advocates, and guardians — anyone navigating the complexities of family court who needs access to credible, empirical, science-based expertise and actionable intelligence.

What Membership Does Not Include

Does membership include legal advice?

+

No. IOFS does not provide legal advice or create an attorney–client relationship. All content provided through The Just Science Community is educational and policy-informed only and does not replace the advice of a licensed legal professional.

Does membership include therapy or diagnosis?

+

No. Membership does not provide treatment, diagnosis, or clinical care. All materials are for educational purposes only and do not create a doctor-patient relationship.

Can IOFS review my individual case through membership?

+

Not through membership. Case review requires a separate formal professional engagement. If you need Dr. Bandy Lee, M.D. to conduct a comprehensive forensic evaluation, peer review, or provide expert testimony in your specific case, please visit our Forensic Services page to request a consultation.

Should membership guide my legal or clinical decisions?

+

No. Materials provided through The Just Science Community are for educational purposes only and do not replace licensed professional advice. We strongly encourage all members to work with qualified legal and clinical professionals in their individual matters.

Pricing & Billing

How much does membership cost?

+

Membership is $129 per month, with full access to all member education, analysis, session recordings, newsletters, and the ability to submit questions across 15 categories of forensic expertise.

Are there long-term contracts?

+

No. Membership is billed monthly with cancellation available per checkout terms. There is no long-term commitment required.

Why The Just Science Community

Why did IOFS create this membership?

+

The founders of IOFS created this membership as an accessible and impactful solution to empower families, advocates, legal professionals, and policymakers with world-class forensic science expertise, strategic education, and critical national data. This level of insight and advocacy support has historically been unattainable for most families due to the significant financial barriers of engaging top-tier forensic professionals — costs that can run into the tens of thousands of dollars. IOFS membership changes that, putting mission-driven science and data-driven advocacy within reach for those who need it most.

Why is membership so critical today?

+

In an era of escalating misinformation and a growing global disconnect from scientific fact, children and families are increasingly placed in peril by decisions that ignore evidence-based forensic science. IOFS membership exists to close that gap — delivering world-renowned expertise, critical national data, and mission-driven education to those navigating a system that too often fails to prioritize truth, safety, and accountability.

Chief Medical Officer

Who is Dr. Bandy X. Lee, M.D.?

Dr. Bandy X. Lee is a Harvard and Yale trained world-renowned forensic and social psychiatrist, violence prevention expert, and global authority on public health approaches to violence. She served on the faculties of Yale School of Medicine and Yale Law School for 17 years before joining Harvard Medical School.

Dr. Lee has pioneered a national movement for family court accountability and reform, engaging dozens of states to take meaningful action. She is the most sought-after forensic psychiatrist globally, and her leadership of IOFS represents a once-in-a-generation opportunity for families to access the highest level of scientific authority.
