What is Forensic Psychiatry?

Forensic psychiatry is the subspecialty of psychiatry dealing with the critical interface between mental health science and the law. Forensic psychiatrists serve as consultants and expert witnesses for civil and criminal attorneys, the judiciary, government agencies, and corporations.

A forensic psychiatric evaluation often carries great weight with judges and juries in resolving family, civil, and criminal matters. When conducted with the highest standards of scientific rigor and ethical integrity, it can be the deciding factor in protecting a child's future.

At IOFS, every evaluation meets the highest threshold of clinical accuracy, legal admissibility, and ethical responsibility — because the stakes for families and children are too high for anything less.
